# AI Usage Policy

Version: 1.0  
Owner: AI Governance Committee  
Last Updated: 2026-03-08  

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the organisational policy governing the development, deployment, and use of artificial intelligence (AI) systems.

The objectives of this policy are to:

- ensure responsible AI usage
- protect sensitive information
- manage operational and regulatory risk
- ensure transparency and accountability in AI-driven decision making

All employees and contractors must comply with this policy when developing or using AI-enabled systems.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- machine learning models
- generative AI systems
- large language models (LLMs)
- automated decision systems
- AI-assisted development tools

The policy applies to both:

- internally developed AI systems
- externally hosted AI services

---

# 3. Core AI Governance Principles

All AI systems must follow these core governance principles.

## 3.1 Accountability

Every AI system must have a clearly defined system owner responsible for:

- system oversight
- operational performance
- compliance with internal policies

Ownership must be documented prior to production deployment.

---

## 3.2 Transparency

AI-enabled decisions must be explainable to an appropriate level.

Where AI systems influence business decisions, the system must provide:

- decision context
- input data sources
- model limitations

Users must be informed when interacting with AI-generated outputs.

---

## 3.3 Risk Management

AI systems must undergo risk assessment prior to deployment.

Risk assessment must consider:

- data sensitivity
- potential bias
- operational failure scenarios
- regulatory compliance obligations

High-risk systems must be reviewed by the AI Governance Committee.

---

## 3.4 Human Oversight

AI systems must not operate without appropriate human supervision in high-impact scenarios.

Human review is required when:

- AI recommendations influence financial decisions
- AI outputs affect customer outcomes
- automated actions may create operational risk

---

# 4. Acceptable Use of AI Systems

Employees may use approved AI tools to support productivity and innovation.

Examples of acceptable use include:

- software development assistance
- documentation drafting
- research summarisation
- internal knowledge retrieval

All usage must comply with data protection requirements.

---

# 5. Prohibited Use of AI Systems

The following activities are strictly prohibited:

- submitting confidential customer data to external AI systems without approval
- generating automated decisions without oversight in regulated processes
- deploying AI systems without architecture review
- bypassing organisational security controls using AI tools

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

---

# 6. Data Protection Requirements

AI systems must comply with organisational data protection policies.

Sensitive data must not be provided to external AI services unless the service has been approved by the security team.

Examples of sensitive data include:

- customer personal information
- financial records
- internal proprietary algorithms
- confidential business strategies

All AI systems must implement appropriate logging and auditing of data usage.

---

# 7. Model Monitoring and Logging

AI systems deployed in production must implement monitoring capabilities.

Monitoring requirements include:

- tracking model performance
- detecting abnormal behaviour
- logging prompts and outputs where appropriate

Monitoring helps ensure AI systems remain reliable and safe over time.

---

# 8. Security Considerations

AI systems must be protected against common AI-specific threats.

These include:

- prompt injection attacks
- data leakage through model responses
- model misuse by internal users

Security controls must be designed to mitigate these risks.

---

# 9. Model Lifecycle Management

AI systems must follow a defined lifecycle.

Typical lifecycle stages include:

1. design and risk assessment
2. development and testing
3. controlled deployment
4. monitoring and maintenance
5. retirement or replacement

Changes to production models must follow change management procedures.

---

# 10. AI Governance Review

Before production deployment, AI systems must undergo architecture and governance review.

Review includes evaluation of:

- model design
- data sources
- security controls
- operational monitoring

The review process ensures that AI systems meet organisational standards.

---

# 11. Reporting and Escalation

Employees must report any concerns regarding AI system behaviour.

Examples include:

- unexpected or unsafe outputs
- suspected bias in model behaviour
- data privacy violations

Reports should be escalated to the AI Governance Committee or security team.

---

# 12. Policy Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory.

Periodic audits may be conducted to ensure adherence to AI governance requirements.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in corrective action.

---

# 13. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually to ensure alignment with:

- evolving regulatory requirements
- industry best practices
- organisational risk tolerance
